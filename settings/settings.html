Getting Started

1

Pin the Extension

Click the puzzle piece icon (Extensions) in the top-right of your browser, then click the pin icon next to Symbar so it's always visible in your toolbar.

2

Open the Toolbar

Click the Symbar icon in your browser toolbar on any webpage. The symbol toolbar will appear at the bottom of the page.

3

Browse or Search

Use the dropdown to switch between categories (French, Math, Greek, Arrows, Currency, Common) or type in the search bar to find any symbol by name.

4

Copy a Symbol

Click any symbol to copy it to your clipboard. Then paste it wherever you need it with Ctrl+V (or Cmd+V on Mac).

5

Quick Popup

Enable the Quick Popup in Settings to see your recently used symbols in a small floating panel whenever you click into a text field.

Tips

  • Your recently used symbols appear first for quick access
  • Add custom symbols in Settings for frequently used text or characters
  • Use the search bar to find symbols by description (e.g., "infinity", "arrow", "degree")
  • Minimize the toolbar to a thin bar when you don't need it, but want it handy
  • The toolbar works on any webpage - just click the extension icon

Do you collect or sell my data?

No. Symbar does not collect, store, or transmit any of your data. All preferences are stored locally in your browser using Chrome's sync storage.

Which browsers are supported?

Symbar works in all Chromium-based browsers including Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, Brave, Vivaldi, and Opera.

What permissions does Symbar use?

Symbar uses two permissions:

  • tabs — Required to inject the toolbar into the active webpage when you click the extension icon.
  • storage — Saves your preferences (theme, toolbar height, custom symbols, recently used) so they persist across sessions and sync across your devices.

Why doesn't the toolbar appear on some pages?

Browser extensions cannot run on internal browser pages (like chrome:// URLs, the Chrome Web Store, or extension pages). Try it on any regular website.
